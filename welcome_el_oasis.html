Resident Orientation & Community Standards

Lobby
Over the years, and in order to ease the arrival of new owners and tenants, the following information has been assembled for your reference. Owners are asked to ensure their tenants are made aware of community ground rules before taking occupancy.

Property Overview

◆There are 15 residential units, each comprising two levels.
◆A unit may be owned by one owner, or each level owned separately. Each unit holds one vote; for informal votes, there is one vote per level.
◆The driveway is for the use of the ground-floor unit. Driveway gates must be locked at night for the security of all residents.
◆Common areas include the front and back parking lots, the pool, and all grounds. No structures may be erected in any common area by owners.
◆Any changes to a building must be accepted by all owners of that building and approved by the HOA.

Governance & Meetings

◆Annual meetings are normally held the third Monday of January, announced a minimum of 30 days in advance.
◆Votes must be announced in advance to allow time for proxy discussions with owners.
◆The HOA currently has 5 elected members/directors, elected annually. This is a voluntary position and your support is appreciated.

Pool & Common Areas

◆Only owners, guests staying at their unit, registered renters, and accompanied guests are permitted use of the pool and common areas.
◆Access to the pool requires the presence of the owner, their houseguests, or their tenants. Short-term registered renters may not bring independent guests to the pool.
◆Showering before entering the pool is obligatory. Jumping and diving are not permitted. Owners are encouraged to lead by example.
◆Do not reserve pool chairs by leaving towels on them.
◆Music is not permitted in the pool common area.

Noise & Conduct

◆Out of respect for all residents, unreasonably loud noise or activity after 10:00 pm — including outdoor parties and amplified music — is not permitted on the premises.
◆No animals of any kind are allowed on the property.
◆Plants and materials on owners' porches are to be maintained by owners, not by the grounds staff.

Waste & Grounds

◆Garbage must be placed directly in the rear bin in hermetically sealed containers. Refuse left out attracts scavengers, insects, and rodents and scatters throughout the property.

Bylaw Extracts — Selected Provisions

Use of Individual Property Each owner shall use his individual property in an orderly and quiet manner and not carry out any act that disturbs the tranquillity of other co-owners, or that compromises the soundness, safety, health, comfort, and order of the building and the condominium as a whole.
Common Area Improvements Ornamental improvements in common areas require the unanimous consent of co-owners. Useful improvements require approval of not less than seventy-five percent of the total value of the condominium. Necessary improvements shall be made with the agreement of a simple majority.
Interior Works & Structural Prohibitions Owners may carry out works or repairs inside their unit; however, any innovation or modification affecting the structure, master walls, or other essential elements of the building or apartments is prohibited. No change or alteration, whether in common or private areas, may affect structural walls or columns, or modify façades.
Front Garden Works Any masonry work in the front gardens — terraces, flower pots, sculpture slabs, lanterns, or other installations — must be consulted with the Administrator prior to execution.
Parking Areas No fences or obstacles may be placed between parking spaces in the parking areas.
Prohibited Materials Enclosure with zinc flashing, black or galvanized iron, or any material that deteriorates the overall appearance of the complex is prohibited under any circumstances.
Exterior Maintenance & Painting Window frames, doors, and light materials that require painting must be kept in good condition. Exterior painting shall not alter the façade colours without the consent of the Manager. Any deteriorated exterior elements — glass, window frames, and similar — must be replaced with materials identical to the originals; under no circumstances may they be replaced with materials of lesser quality.
Animals No animals of any kind shall be allowed.
